The Origin Story

From Lagos, Nigeria to the UFC Octagon. Now building champions in your backyard.

Sodiq Yusuff did not have an easy path. He came to America from Lagos, Nigeria at nine years old. No language. No connections. No roadmap. Just a kid trying to figure out a country that was not built for him. He got bullied. He found wrestling. And then something clicked that never turned off.

His mother did not know he was competing. He fought eleven amateur fights before she ever watched. He was working at Pizza Hut, attending Bowie State, trying to make his mother happy while secretly chasing a dream that everyone around him thought was impossible.

Then Master Lloyd Irvin — UFC Hall of Fame coach — called.

He earned his UFC contract on Dana White's Contender Series. He made his UFC debut in Australia — one week after learning his older brother Tope had died in Nigeria. His mother had hidden the news to protect his preparation. He won by first-round knockout. He dedicated it to Tope.

Since then he has competed on UFC 300. He has earned Fight of the Night. He has voiced a character in Disney's Iwaju. He has built a TikTok following of 1.6 million people.

And now — for the first time in the history of Southern Maryland — an active UFC fighter has opened his own academy here. Not to visit. Not to hold a one-day seminar. To stay. To teach. To build.
