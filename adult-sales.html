What Mixed Martial Arts actually is

What You Actually Learn

This is not a kickboxing cardio class. This is the complete Mixed Martial Arts system.

Boxing — The Foundation of Everything That Happens on Your Feet

Not fitness boxing. Real fundamentals — the jab, the cross, the hook, the uppercut — all built on the platform of footwork, distance management, and head movement. Sodiq's entire striking game at the UFC level is built around his boxing foundation. MMA analysts have called him the cleaner technical striker in every fight he has been in.

You will learn how to hit correctly. How to generate real power from your legs and hips. How to move your head so you are not where the punch is going. How to read and time a counter.

Wrestling and Takedowns — How to Control Any Situation

Wrestling is the foundation of physical control. Sodiq's wrestling background goes all the way back to Bladensburg High School. His 70 percent takedown defense at the UFC level is the direct result of fundamentals drilled over years.

You will learn how to shoot, how to defend, how to use the clinch, and how to use your hips and leverage — not just your strength — to control another person's body.

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Ground Control — How to Stay Calm When Everything Goes Wrong

Sodiq holds a blue belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu under Master Lloyd Irvin — UFC Hall of Fame coach. He used his jiu-jitsu to submit a UFC opponent in 30 seconds with a guillotine choke. He held Andre Fili on the ground for over four minutes in a UFC fight.

You will learn how to move on the ground. How to escape bad positions. How to submit and how to avoid being submitted. And you will develop calmness under pressure that transfers into every stressful situation outside the gym.

Kickboxing and Muay Thai — How to Use Your Whole Body as a Weapon

Sodiq's leg kicks are one of his most recognized weapons at the UFC level. He used them to systematically dismantle Alex Cáceres across three rounds. You will learn how to throw kicks with your hip behind them, how to set them up off your boxing combinations, how to use the Thai clinch, and the Yoza Kick — a technique Sodiq introduced to UFC audiences.

Real Self-Defense — What Happens When You Actually Need It

If someone grabs you — you will know what to do. If someone swings at you — you will not freeze. If a situation goes to the ground — you will not panic. The skills built in this program are drilled into your nervous system through repetition until they are automatic.
